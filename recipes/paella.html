Paella

Ingredients Steps

Description

Paella is a classic Spanish dish that originated in Valencia. This stunning one-pan meal features saffron-infused rice cooked with seafood, vegetables, and aromatic spices. The signature feature is the crispy, golden layer of rice at the bottom called "socarrat."

Paella is traditionally cooked in a wide, shallow paella pan and served directly from it. The dish is perfect for gatherings, combining bold flavors and beautiful presentation. Each spoonful delivers a perfect mix of tender seafood, fragrant rice, and aromatic saffron.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (Bomba or Arborio)
  • 1 pinch saffron threads
  • 4 cups seafood or vegetable broth
  • 300g shrimp
  • 200g mussels
  • 200g squid, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat the broth in a separate pot and steep the saffron threads in it for at least 10 minutes.
  2. Heat olive oil in a paella pan over medium heat.
  3. Sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the bell peppers and cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Stir in the rice and cook for 2 minutes until lightly toasted.
  6. Add paprika and diced tomatoes, stirring well to combine.
  7. Pour in the saffron-infused broth and distribute evenly. Do not stir after this point.
  8. Arrange the shrimp, mussels, and squid on top of the rice.
  9. Scatter green peas over the top and season with salt and pepper.
  10. Cook uncovered for about 20-25 minutes until the rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  11. Increase heat slightly in the last 2-3 minutes to create the crispy bottom layer (socarrat).
  12. Remove from heat, cover with foil, and let rest for 5 minutes.
  13. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges.
