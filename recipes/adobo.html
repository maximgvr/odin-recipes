Filipino Adobo

Ingredients Steps

Description

Filipino Adobo is one of the most beloved dishes in Filipino cuisine. It features meat (usually chicken or pork) braised slowly in a savory sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. The balance of salty and tangy flavors creates a rich, deeply satisfying dish.

Adobo is versatile and improves with time as flavors develop. It's traditionally served with steamed white rice and is perfect for family gatherings. The leftover sauce makes an excellent condiment and tastes even better the next day.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white or calamansi)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add the soy sauce and vinegar, stirring to combine.
  5. Return the chicken to the pot and add water, bay leaves, and pepper.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer covered for 30-40 minutes.
  7. Add onion slices and continue simmering for 10 more minutes.
  8. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed. Add brown sugar if desired for balance.
  9. Simmer uncovered for the last 5 minutes to reduce the sauce slightly.
  10. Transfer to a serving dish and serve hot with steamed rice.
